# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

B2B decision-makers in Germany (procurement, logistics leads, production managers, e-commerce operators) who currently ship with DHL, DPD, UPS, FedEx, or GLS and feel like a number in an anonymous ticket queue. They need reliable courier/freight service (overnight domestic, European direct runs, international, same-day/emergency) and want a dispatcher who actually knows their operation, not a call center.

## Product Purpose

MAILEXPRESS (MEX) is a Dresden-based courier and logistics company, 24 years in business, positioning itself as the direct, personal alternative to the large anonymous carriers. The site (`/neu/`) needs to convert cold B2B visitors into contact requests or Compass sign-ups by proving: a named dispatcher answers, pricing is transparent and competitive (live carrier price comparison), and the network reach (nationwide overnight, European partners) matches the majors without the impersonal scale.

## Brand Personality

Modern, professional, and technically credible (the Compass platform is a real product, not vaporware) — but never cold or generic-SaaS. Warmth comes through the "fester Disponent" story, real depot photography, and direct phone/email contact, not through playful or startup-coded visual flourishes. Confident, precise, quietly regional (Dresden roots) rather than loud or corporate-anonymous.

## Anti-references

- Not a cold, sterile SaaS tool aesthetic (no dashboard-chrome-for-its-own-sake, no faceless enterprise gloss).
- Not a generic, interchangeable freight-company site (avoid stock corporate blue/gray with no personality).
- Not playful/startup-coded (no bouncy illustrations, no consumer-app whimsy) — audience is B2B procurement, not consumers.

## Design Principles

1. **Personal beats anonymous.** Every section should reinforce that a named human (der Disponent) is behind the service, in contrast to the big carriers' hotline/ticket experience.
2. **Prove the tech, keep the warmth.** Compass (the booking/tracking platform) should look genuinely modern and professional, but always paired with human signals nearby (real photos, direct phone number, named reviewers) so it never reads as a faceless tool.
3. **Price transparency as trust signal.** The live multi-carrier price comparison is a credibility proof point, not just a widget — keep it prominent, accurate, and functional.
4. **Regional roots, national/European reach.** Dresden HQ and 24 years in business are the credibility anchor; the network story should show scale (Germany overnight, EU partners) without losing the local, personal framing.
5. **B2B clarity over consumer flash.** Audience is procurement/logistics/production decision-makers. Favor confident, functional clarity over playful or trend-chasing visual flourishes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific stated requirements; default to WCAG AA (contrast, focus states, keyboard-operable FAQ/nav/price widget). Revisit if the user specifies otherwise.
